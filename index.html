Dedicated Event Management Teams:

F&B TEAM.

Food and Beverage Team

Menu Planning:

3Designing and curating menus that align with the event theme and client preferences.

Vendor Coordination:

Liaising with food and beverage vendors, negotiating contracts, and ensuring timely delivery of supplies.

Staffing:

Recruiting, training, and managing F&B staff, including servers, bartenders, and chefs.

Quality Control:

Ensuring the quality and presentation of food and beverages meet the highest standards.

Budget Management:

Monitoring expenses, controlling costs, and maximizing profitability without compromising quality.

SHADOW TEAM.

Shadow Team With Groom and Bride

Event Support:

Providing behind-the-scenes assistance to ensure smooth event operations, catering specifically to the needs and desires of the groom and bride.

Security:

Managing crowd control, ensuring safety protocols are followed, and addressing any security concerns, with a focus on protecting the privacy and comfort for Groom and Bride.

Logistics:

Handling transportation, equipment setup, and breakdown to support the event, with attention to detail to meet the preferences of the groom and bride.

Communication:

Serving as a point of contact for event organizers and coordinating communication between different teams, while also ensuring the groom and bride are informed and involved in decision-making processes.

Emergency Response:

Being prepared to handle emergencies or unexpected situations during the event, prioritizing the well-being and peace of mind of the groom and bride.

SOUND TEAM.

Sound Management

Audio Setup

Designing and setting up sound systems to meet the acoustical needs of the event space.

Technical Support:

Providing technical expertise for sound equipment operation and troubleshooting.

Music Selection:

Curating playlists or coordinating live performances that enhance the atmosphere of the event.

Sound Checks:

Ensuring the quality and presentation of Sound System meet the highest standards.

Coordination:

Collaborating with other teams to synchronize sound elements with the overall event production.

RITUAL TEAM.

Cultural Understanding:

Researching and understanding the cultural or religious rituals that will be incorporated into the event.

Coordination:

Working closely with event organizers and participants to ensure seamless execution of rituals.

Adaptability:

Being prepared to adapt plans and procedures as needed to accommodate any changes or last-minute requests related to rituals.

HOSPITALITY TEAM.

Guist Management

Guest Services:

Providing exceptional customer service to ensure guest satisfaction throughout the event.

Front-of-House Operations:

Managing reception areas, guest check-in, and coat check services.

VIP Treatment:

Overseeing arrangements for VIP guests, including special accommodations and personalized services.

Hospitality Suites:

Managing hospitality suites and amenities for sponsors, speakers, or other distinguished guests.

Feedback Collection:

Soliciting feedback from guests to identify areas for improvement and ensure future events meet or exceed expectations.

PRODUCTION TEAM.

Handles the setup, operation, and maintenance of audiovisual equipment, stage lighting, and other technical aspects of the event, ensuring everything runs smoothly from a technical standpoint.
